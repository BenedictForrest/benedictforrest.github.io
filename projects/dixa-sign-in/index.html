Improving the Dixa login screen for it's users. And if it happens to benefit us too, who's complaining?

When thinking about ways to improve the Dixa web app our focus went to some of out most important features first. We'd think of things like adding more data to the Dashboard, making more messages visible at once in the Conversation screen, refreshing the look and feel of the UI.

But there was one part of the app, a very important part of the app, that users interacted with every day that we were overlooking. A part of the app so important that if it stopped working no other feature on the app could be accessed. A part of the app staring us in the face nearly every day and yet somehow completely forgotten about.

The sign in screen, duh!

The sign in screen has facilitated hundreds of users signing in to use Dixa at least once a week for many years. We should probably have started a countdown to our one millionth sign in so we could send the lucky user one of those giant, novelty cheques to celebrate.

But just because users weren't sending us angry emails every other day to complain about it, doesn't mean it didn't need improvements. Taking a quick look at the original sign in screen, there's nothing horribly wrong with it. But let's take a closer look...

A closer look

Now, can I tell when a gap is 7px when it should be 8px with the naked eye? Maybe... so developers be forewarned if you submit a ticket for a design review. But if/when the naked eye fails, dev tools pick up the slack. After reviewing the sign in screen in the browser dev tools I found a lot off off-system values being used for things like font sizes, colours, and gaps.

I also discovered areas that needed improvement in off-tone language, a confusing mix up of terminology, and missing accessibility features like placeholder text for inputs. All little things that can be easily fixed now that we were of them. With those details out of the way we could start making some real juicy improvements to the way the sign in process works.

What is the right way to sign in?

Sometimes emails can be slow to arrive, or they might go into a Spam folder. And if a user for some reason lost access to their email that means they lost access to Dixa too.

I worked with our Customer Success team to identify what other types of authentication methods are customers were familiar with and would like to use to access Dixa. Then I worked with the Development team to create the many variations of the UI we would need.

Listening to all of our users

Paying attention to user feedback is critical in the product development cycle. We gathered it through customer satisfaction reviews, social media comments, and in-person and virtual client meetings. But there's another important place I go to get invaluable feedback: the QA team!

Yes, the QA testers use the product more than anyone on the team, and they were signing in upwards of a dozen times a day, as a part of testing bug patches and feature releases. And a unique problem they were running into was not being able to easily tell one client's sign in subdomain from another, as they often had multiple open at once.

To address this issue I added the company's logo and name to the sign in screen, which only appear on their unique subdomain, rapha.dixa.com for example. While this change was mainly aimed at improving our experience, it would go on to help our bigger clients who had multi-domain customer support accounts.

The results

John Wooden, and American basketball coach said ‚ÄúIt's the little details that are vital. Little things make big things happen.‚Äù That's what this project was about, the little things. You don't need to do a complete redesign of your application to make it feel fresh. You don't need to rebuild your codebase from the group up to make it faster, but it might help.

By making small adjustments, like ensuring adherence to the design system tokens and writing style guide through design reviews; making signing in quicker and more accessible through new authentication methods; we made a meaningful improvement to the usability of our app.
